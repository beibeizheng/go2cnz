DEAL OF THE WEEK CONTACT US
New Zealand's southernmost 7-day tour

Invercargill, Bill Richardson Transport World, Stewart Island, Oban, Ulva Island, Rakiura Trail, and much more…Enjoy some southern hospitality as we venture to the very bottom of the South Island. Flying first to Invercargill, we explore our southern most city before heading to Stewart Island the next day by plane. Our island visit is the highlight of our trip as we immerse ourselves in the scenery, fresh food and local culture.

#DEAL OF THE WEEK IN SOUTHERMOST

Day1-Day2 A short city tour & Bill Richardson Transport World
Day3-Day4 Oban’s town & Rakiura Great Walk
Day5 Rakiura Museum & Bunkehouse Theatre
Day6 Go to Ulva Island
Day7 Fishing or Play golf

Day1 - Day2: A short city tour & Bill Richardson Transport World

On the first day, after arriving in Invercargill from various locations, a short city tour of New Zealand‘s southernmost city can be taken, including a visit to the famous ’Burt Munro‘ exhibition at the iconic E. Hayes shop. The next day, take a drive to the amazing Bill Richardson Transport World, a real Southland icon.

Day3 – Day4 : Oban’s town & Rakiura Great Walk

After a 20-minute flight from Auckland to Oban, the focus of the day is on getting acquainted with the area, walking around the town's scenic beaches and interesting local shops, and enjoying an island dinner in the evening at a beautiful waterfront restaurant.The next day can be spent hiking the Rakiura Trail, one of the Great Walks - New Zealand's premier hiking trails that pass through some of the most awe-inspiring and pristine landscapes, choose how long the trail is depending on your interests.

Day5:Rakiura Museum & Bunkehouse Theatre

Today is mostly about immersing yourself in the relaxed atmosphere of the island. In the morning, you can visit the island's new Rakiura Museum, which houses an extensive collection of objects and photographs of Stewart Island's early history, including Maori settlement, sheep hunting, whaling, timber processing, mining, shipbuilding, transport, schools and churches. In the afternoon, you can visit the Bunkehouse Theatre, one of Oban's famous attractions.

Day6: Go to Ulva Island

The sixth day we'll board a catamaran and head to Ulva Island - a bird sanctuary in Paterson Bay. As we cruise through sheltered waters, keep an eye out for the numerous seals and penguins in the area. On the island, we walk easily through virgin forest to admire the reserve and its flora and fauna, which is home to many rare and endangered birds.

Day7: Fishing or Play golf

If you like to fish, on the last day of your trip you can take one of the many small private fishing charters. Go out for 3 or 4 hours (morning and afternoon options) and see if you can catch the island‘s large numbers of blue whiting. If you like to play golf, play a round of golf on New Zealand's most remote golf course.
